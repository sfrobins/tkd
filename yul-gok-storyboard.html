Yul-Gok Tul 율곡 틀  ·  栗谷

Named after the great Korean philosopher and scholar Yi I (1536–1584), whose pen name was Yul-Gok — the "Confucius of Korea." The 38 movements represent his birthplace on the 38th parallel, and the pattern diagram traces the Chinese character 士 (scholar), embodying the union of intellect and martial discipline.
